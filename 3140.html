Embedded Systems

I helped transition the Cornell Embedded Systems course (ECE 3140) to an ARM Processor. This transition involved adapting the 5 labs in the course to use the ARM instruction set, as well as writing documentation and lab handouts for the students. The course begins with assembly languages (now the Thumb-2 instruction set) and builds up to locks, schedulers, and context switching. The further labs are all coded in C.

The Labs

Lab 1: Introduces assembly code, uses low level commands to blink LEDs and manage a timer.
Lab 2: Explores clock gating and I/O through both polling and interrupts.
Lab 3: Introduces concurrency by time-sharing multiple processes.
Lab 4: Using locks, creates a scheduler which expands upon Lab 3.

Final Project

For the last few weeks of the semester, the students choose a final project to implement using the microcontroller from the class. When I took the class, a partner and I implemented MatchSP-430, a dating app based on the MSP430 processor. This application allowed users to create a profile locally (using a UART serial connection) and then communicate and match with other users via the on-chip radio.
