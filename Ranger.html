The Project

The Cornell Ranger, constructed in 2011-2012 in the Biorobotics and Locomotion Lab under Professor Ruina, is a robot designed to be incredibly energy-efficient. It is able to walk over 40 miles on a single charge. Unfortunately, the original control system was flimsy at best, and the Cornell ranger had no way of starting or stopping without human intervention. A Ph.D. student chose to redesign the control system as his thesis, and enlisted myself and another undergraduate student to code the robot.
With simulations and mathematical models provided by our Ph.D. student, we implemented and commented all of the code necessary for the robot to walk.
